About

My name is Ehud Tsivion or simply "Udi". I'm a computational scientist - I did my PhD in physical chemistry at the Hebrew University in Jerusalem and later worked as a postdoc at UC Berkeley.

In the past, I've built physical models and used computers and algorithms to simulate physical processes such desorption of molecules from a surface or dissociation of weakly bound clusters.

I'm passionate about problems that have impact on daily lives and have spent my recent years trying to figure out how to design hydrogen-gas adsorbing materials that would enable non-polluting vehicles.

My favourite atom is xenon. It's really awesome.
