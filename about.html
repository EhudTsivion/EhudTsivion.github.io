About Me

My name is Ehud Tsivion, but everyone calls me Udi.

I live in Berkeley, California but my home is in Modi'in, Israel.

I work at the Department of Chemistry, University of California, Berkeley and Materials Sciences Division, Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory.

I am a Theoretical/Computational chemist interested in real-world problems.

Short Bio

As a graduate student with Benny Gerber at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, I explored the chemistry of the noble-gas containing molecules of the H-Ng-Y family.

Now, as a postdoc at UC Berkeley, I'm working with Martin Head-Gordon on the theory of adsorption and storage of H2 in Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs).
